    Gene Hackman: Bio, Career and Marriages

    Gene Hackman had an illustrious career spanning over six decades, establishing himself as one of the greatest actors of his generation.

    He received numerous accolades throughout his career, including two Academy Awards, two BAFTA Awards, four Golden Globe Awards, a Screen Actors Guild Award, and the Silver Bear.

    Hackman initially found success in the theater, showcasing his talent in productions like The Cherry Orchard and Death of a Salesman.

    He transitioned to the big screen in 1964 with his film debut in Lilith.

    In 1971, Hackman gained widespread recognition for his role as Detective Jimmy “Popeye” Doyle in the film The French Connection.

    His portrayal of a tough New York City cop determined to take down a drug kingpin earned him an Academy Award for Best Actor.

    Throughout the 1970s, Hackman continued to deliver remarkable performances in notable films such as The Conversation, Superman, and I Never Sang for My Father.

    He received his second Academy Award for Best Actor for his role in Unforgiven in 1992.

    In 2004, Hackman decided to retire from acting. Since then, he has pursued his passion for writing and has authored two novels, Wake of the Perdido Star and A Death in the Family.

    Net Worth and Salary

    Gene Hackman boasts a net worth of $80 million.

    Throughout his impressive nearly 50-year career, Hackman earned over 30 awards, including two Academy Awards, four Golden Globes, and two BAFTAs.

    His portrayal of Buck Barrow in the 1967 film “Bonnie and Clyde” served as a career catalyst.

    Personal Life

    Gene married Faye Maltese on January 1, 1956. They had three children together: Christopher, Elizabeth, and Leslie.

    However, they divorced in 1986. Later, in December 1991, he married Betsy Arakawa.

    Gene has actively participated in various racing competitions, including the 24 Hours of Daytona Endurance Race and the Long Beach Grand Prix Celebrity Race.

    In 1990, while vacationing in Oregon, Gene experienced chest pains and underwent an angioplasty.

    Additionally, in 2012, while riding a bike in the Florida Keys, he was struck by a car.

    Nominations, Awards and Honors

    Gene Hackman has been nominated for many awards throughout his career, including:

    • Academy Awards: Hackman has been nominated for five Academy Awards, winning two. He won Best Actor for his role in The French Connection (1971) and Best Supporting Actor for his role in Unforgiven (1992).
    • Golden Globe Awards: Hackman has won four Golden Globe Awards, including two for Best Actor – Motion Picture Drama. He won for his roles in The French Connection (1971) and Unforgiven (1992).
    • BAFTA Awards: Hackman has won two BAFTA Awards, including one for Best Actor. He won for his role in The French Connection (1971).
    • Screen Actors Guild Awards: Hackman has won one Screen Actors Guild Award, for Outstanding Performance by a Male Actor in a Leading Role. He won for his role in Unforgiven (1992).
    • Cecil B. DeMille Award: Hackman was awarded the Cecil B. DeMille Award in 2003 for his lifetime achievement in motion pictures.

    In addition to his awards and nominations, Hackman has also received numerous other honors, including:

    • Kennedy Center Honor: Hackman was awarded the Kennedy Center Honor in 2013 for his lifetime achievement in the performing arts.
    • National Medal of Arts: Hackman was awarded the National Medal of Arts in 2017 by President Barack Obama.
    • American Film Institute: The American Film Institute has ranked Hackman as the 24th greatest male actor of all time.
